# Fieldwork Survey Plan

## Scope

The current planning scenario covers approximately 400 schools across five regions:

- Santiago: 160 schools
- Four additional regions: 60 schools each

The survey is planned for four grades in each school. The working assumption is that a two-person team can cover the required classrooms in a school visit and can complete about three nearby schools in a full field day.

## Staffing Plan

Each field team has two enumerators.

Current team allocation:

- Santiago: 4 teams
- Each other region: 2 teams
- Total across all regions: 12 teams
- Total enumerators: 24

Daily capacity under the three-schools-per-team-day assumption:

- Santiago: 4 teams x 3 schools = 12 schools per field day
- Each other region: 2 teams x 3 schools = 6 schools per field day

Expected active field days:

- Santiago: 160 schools / 12 schools per day = about 14 field days
- Each other region: 60 schools / 6 schools per day = about 10 field days

Because all regions can operate in parallel, the main fieldwork duration is driven by Santiago, at roughly 14 active field days. A six-week fieldwork window gives room for scheduling constraints, travel time, school cancellations, revisits, and data-quality checks.

## Enumerator Payment Structure

Enumerators are paid:

- USD 14 for each school visited
- USD 8 for each class interviewed
- USD 10 for average transport costs per field day
- USD 20 as a one-time training payment

With four grades surveyed per school and two enumerators per team, the working assumption is that each enumerator interviews two classes per school.

Expected payment per school per enumerator:

```text
USD 14 school visit payment + 2 classes x USD 8 = USD 30 per school
```

Expected payment per complete field day, assuming three schools per day and a USD 10 daily transport allowance:

```text
3 schools x USD 30 + USD 10 transport = USD 100 per field day
```

So a complete field day pays about USD 100 per enumerator, including the average transport allowance.

## Expected Enumerator Workload and Compensation

An enumerator hired for the equivalent of 10 to 15 field days would cover approximately 30 to 45 school visits with their team.

Expected compensation:

```text
10 field days x USD 100 = USD 1,000
15 field days x USD 100 = USD 1,500
```

Thus, expected enumerator compensation plus transport allowance is roughly USD 1,000 to USD 1,500 for covering about 30 to 45 schools.

The one-time training payment adds USD 20 per enumerator.

## Approximate Total Enumerator Cost

For 400 schools, two enumerators per school visit, and four classes per school:

School-visit payments:

```text
400 schools x 2 enumerators x USD 14 = USD 11,200
```

Class-interview payments:

```text
400 schools x 4 classes x USD 8 = USD 12,800
```

Transport payments:

```text
400 schools / 3 schools per team-day x 2 enumerators x USD 10 = about USD 2,667
```

Training payments:

```text
24 enumerators x USD 20 = USD 480
```

Approximate total enumerator payment:

```text
USD 11,200 + USD 12,800 + USD 2,667 + USD 480 = about USD 27,147
```

This figure covers direct enumerator payments under the current assumptions. It does not include supervisor time, transport, lodging, training, survey materials, incentives, replacement visits, or administrative overhead.

## Key Planning Assumptions

- Each team has two enumerators.
- Each school visit covers four grades/classes.
- Each enumerator interviews two classes per school.
- A team can complete three nearby schools in a full field day.
- Santiago has 160 sampled schools.
- Each of four other regions has 60 sampled schools.
- Regions operate in parallel during the fieldwork window.
- The six-week window is intended to include buffer time, not just active interviewing days.
